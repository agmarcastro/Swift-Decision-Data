# KPI Definitions — InfoAgent Knowledge Base

This document defines the business KPIs used by the technology retail company.
All formulas reference the Star Schema column names exactly as they appear in the database.

---

## 1. Gross Profit Margin (%)

**What it measures:** The percentage of revenue retained after deducting the cost of goods sold.
A higher margin indicates better pricing power and cost control.

**Formula:**
```
Gross Profit Margin (%) = (SUM(valor_total) - SUM(custo_total)) / SUM(valor_total) * 100
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.valor_total`, `fato_vendas.custo_total`

**Interpretation:** Industry benchmark for technology retail is 20–30%. Below 15% signals margin pressure.

---

## 2. GMROI — Gross Margin Return on Investment

**What it measures:** How many reais of gross margin the company earns for every real invested in inventory.
Used to evaluate whether inventory investment is generating adequate return.

**Formula:**
```
GMROI = Gross Margin / Average Inventory Cost

Where:
  Gross Margin = SUM(valor_total) - SUM(custo_total)  [from fato_vendas]
  Average Inventory Cost = AVG(qtd_disponivel * unit_cost)  [from fato_estoque joined to fato_vendas for unit cost]
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.valor_total`, `fato_vendas.custo_total`, `fato_estoque.qtd_disponivel`

**Interpretation:** GMROI > 1.0 means the inventory is generating more gross margin than it costs to hold.

---

## 3. Category Revenue Share (%)

**What it measures:** The percentage of total company revenue contributed by a specific product department or category.

**Formula:**
```
Category Revenue Share (%) = SUM(valor_total WHERE departamento = X) / SUM(valor_total) * 100
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.valor_total`, `dim_produto.departamento`, `dim_produto.categoria`

**Interpretation:** Identifies the most and least commercially significant product lines.

---

## 4. Inventory Turnover / DSI — Days Sales of Inventory

**What it measures:** How many days it takes to sell through the current inventory.
A lower DSI means inventory is turning faster (more efficient).

**Formula:**
```
DSI (Days Sales of Inventory) = (Average Inventory / COGS) * 365

Where:
  Average Inventory = AVG(qtd_disponivel)  [from fato_estoque for the period]
  COGS = SUM(custo_total)  [from fato_vendas for the same period]
```

**Columns:** `fato_estoque.qtd_disponivel`, `fato_vendas.custo_total`

**Interpretation:** DSI < 30 days is healthy for fast-moving electronics. DSI > 90 days indicates overstocking.

---

## 5. Out-of-Stock (OOS) Rate (%)

**What it measures:** The percentage of active SKUs with zero available inventory at a given point in time.
A high OOS rate means lost sales opportunities.

**Formula:**
```
OOS Rate (%) = COUNT(id_produto WHERE qtd_disponivel = 0) / COUNT(DISTINCT id_produto) * 100
```

**Columns:** `fato_estoque.qtd_disponivel`, `fato_estoque.id_produto`

**Interpretation:** OOS Rate > 5% is considered problematic. Anything above 10% requires immediate replenishment action.
The standard stockout threshold is qtd_disponivel < 5 units for high-velocity SKUs.

---

## 6. Sell-Through Rate (%)

**What it measures:** The percentage of received inventory that has been sold in a given period.
Used to evaluate product velocity and buying decisions.

**Formula:**
```
Sell-Through Rate (%) = SUM(quantidade sold) / (SUM(quantidade sold) + SUM(qtd_disponivel)) * 100
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.quantidade`, `fato_estoque.qtd_disponivel`

**Interpretation:** Above 80% is excellent. Below 50% indicates slow-moving inventory risk.

---

## 7. Average Order Value (AOV)

**What it measures:** The average revenue generated per sales transaction.

**Formula:**
```
AOV = SUM(valor_total) / COUNT(DISTINCT id_venda)
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.valor_total`, `fato_vendas.id_venda`

**Interpretation:** Increasing AOV is a key objective for upselling and cross-selling initiatives.

---

## 8. Units Per Transaction (UPT)

**What it measures:** The average number of product units sold per transaction.
A proxy for basket size and cross-sell effectiveness.

**Formula:**
```
UPT = SUM(quantidade) / COUNT(DISTINCT id_venda)
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.quantidade`, `fato_vendas.id_venda`

**Interpretation:** UPT > 1.5 in the Mobile category suggests effective accessory attachment.

---

## 9. Loyalty Program Contribution (%)

**What it measures:** The percentage of total revenue generated by loyalty club members (Bronze, Silver, or Gold tier).

**Formula:**
```
Loyalty Program Contribution (%) =
  SUM(fv.valor_total WHERE dc.categoria_clube_info IN ('Bronze','Silver','Gold'))
  / SUM(fv.valor_total) * 100
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.valor_total`, `dim_cliente.categoria_clube_info`

**Interpretation:** A higher percentage indicates a healthy loyalty program. Target > 40% of revenue from loyalty members.

---

## 10. Attachment Rate / Cross-Sell Rate

**What it measures:** The percentage of primary product purchases that include at least one accessory or complementary product in the same transaction.

**Formula:**
```
Attachment Rate (%) =
  COUNT(DISTINCT transactions that include both a primary product AND a complementary product)
  / COUNT(DISTINCT transactions with the primary product) * 100

Implementation: Use a self-join on fato_vendas by id_venda to find transactions
with products from multiple categories.
```

**Columns:** `fato_vendas.id_venda`, `dim_produto.departamento`, `dim_produto.categoria`

**Interpretation:** In the Mobile category, an attachment rate > 30% for accessories (headphones, cases) is the target.
